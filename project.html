Email Security

Technology Used: Java

Description:

A java-based email client that uses end-to-end encryption for encrypting emails.The main reasons behind implementing this project is to reap benefits from Gmail that protects our data from external attackers, and also protect our confidential data from Gmail itself. The body of the email is encrypted, as a result email service providers (such as Gmail) will not be able to read our conversations and hence complete privacy of user emails will be maintained. To put a perspective, client side encryption and decryption is performed and if one opens Gmail from web-browser all the emails will be seem gibberish as the body of the email is encrypted indicating that even if Gmail decides to read our emails, no meaningful information can be recovered. Only our Java application that is residing on the client-side will be able to decrypt email for the user. It goes without mentioning that even if the email service provider is compromised, your data will still be secure. For implementing the solution we have used RSA public-private key pairs along with cryptographic algorithms such as AES-symmetric encryption, HMAC, RSA-public key encryption (OAEP) and PBKDF2.

Data Analysis using R-programming

Technology Used: R-programming

Description:

I have maintained a repository of all the R-programs that I have implemented in order to analyze various datasets and identify patterns in them. I have a good-knowledge of Support Vector Machines for data-classification as I have classified a couple of datasets using the same. Datasets that have been analyzed range from basic ones such as the iris dataset to more complex ones such as sonar datasets. I have also implemented features of the Caret Package such as Data Partitioning, Data training and Data prediction in R using the open source data-sets available at the UCI repository. One of the data-set that I analyzed was of Sonar signals where it was required to distinguish between signals bounced off a metal cylinder and those off a roughly cylindrical rock and then examine the model's prediction on a separate test-set. The other data-set was about classifying leaves according to their species depending upon 14 attributes provided by the data-set and then again testing the model on a separate test-set. I evaluated each of the data-sets using "Random Forests", "Gradient Boosting Machines" and "Partial Least Squares regression" classification/regression models and analysed the accuracy of each of them. Apart from this there are more assignments related to data-modelling and predications using R-programming language available at my GitHub Repository.

Source code: R-programming

Technology Used: Python

Description:

Under the guidance of Prof. Oscar Morales Ponce, we are currently performing research to determine fundamental problems associated with the Torus Networks.
